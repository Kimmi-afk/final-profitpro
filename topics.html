Product/Service Details

• Product/Service Name: Name of the product or service being priced.

• Description: Brief description of the product or service.

Example:

• Product Name: "Smartphone X Pro"

• Description: "High-end smartphone with advanced features like 5G, AI camera, and premium design."

Cost Structure

• Cost of Goods Sold (COGS): The direct cost of producing the product or service.

• Fixed Costs: Overhead or fixed costs that don't vary with production (e.g., rent, salaries).

• Variable Costs: Costs that vary with production or sales (e.g., shipping, commissions).

Example:

• COGS: $250

• Fixed Costs: $50,000 per month

• Variable Costs: $10 per unit

Target Metrics

• Desired Profit Margin (%): The profit margin the user wants to achieve.

• Revenue Target: The total revenue the user hopes to generate within a certain timeframe (optional for more complex simulations).

• Market Share Target: Percentage of the market that the user wants to capture (for comparative analysis).

Example:

• Desired Profit Margin: 30%

• Revenue Target: $500,000

• Market Share Target: 10%

Pricing Strategy Decisions (Dropdown/Radio Buttons)

• Cost-Plus Pricing: Price is based on the cost of goods sold plus a markup.

• Dynamic Pricing: Pricing that changes based on demand, competitor pricing, or other factors.

• Value-Based Pricing: Pricing based on the perceived value to the customer.

• Penetration Pricing: Initially setting a low price to capture market share and increase later.

• Price Skimming: Setting a high initial price and gradually lowering it over time.

Example:

• Selected Strategy: "Cost-Plus Pricing"

Price Sensitivity Factors (For Dynamic or Value-Based Pricing)

• Elasticity of Demand: How sensitive customer demand is to price changes (price elasticity).

• Competitor Pricing: Pricing of competitors’ similar products.

• Customer Perception of Value: How customers perceive the value of the product (on a scale from 1 to 10, for example).

Example:

• Elasticity of Demand: -1.2 (indicating somewhat elastic demand)

• Competitor Pricing: $999

• Customer Perception of Value: 8/10v

Discounts and Promotions

• Discount Type: Options like percentage discount, fixed amount off, or promotional pricing.

• Discount Percentage: For percentage-based discounts.

• Discount Amount: For fixed discount amounts.

• Promotional Period: How long the discount or promotion lasts.

Example:

• Discount Type: "Percentage Discount"

• Discount Percentage: 15%

• Promotional Period: 30 days

Sales Volume Estimates

• Estimated Sales Volume: Expected number of units to be sold at the selected price.

• Sales Growth Rate: The expected growth rate in sales over time (optional for long-term simulations).

Example:

• Estimated Sales Volume: 10,000 units/month

• Sales Growth Rate: 5% per month

Revenue and Cost Assumptions

• Currency: To determine the correct units of measurement (USD, EUR, etc.).

• Pricing Frequency: Whether the price is set per unit, per subscription period, etc.

Seasonality Factors: Adjustments for seasonality in demand (optional).

Example:

• Currency: USD

• Pricing Frequency: Per unit (one-time purchase)

• Seasonality Factors: High demand in Q4 (holiday season)

Advanced Variables for Simulation

• Break-even Point: Option to set or display the break-even point.

• Competitor Response (Dynamic Pricing): An estimated response rate from competitors to price changes (optional).

• Pricing Tiers: For multi-tiered pricing models (e.g., Basic, Premium, Enterprise).

Example:

• Break-even Point: 2,000 units

Competitor Response: Price increase of 5% for a 10% price increase in the product.
